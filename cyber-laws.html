Global Cyber Law Overview

Cyber law refers to the body of law that regulates the internet, technology, and electronic communications. It aims to protect individuals, businesses, and governments from cybercrimes, data breaches, and other online threats.

Cyber Laws by Region

United States

The USA has several cyber laws, including the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA), which criminalises hacking, and the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), which ensures the protection of personal health information.

European Union

The EU’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is one of the most comprehensive data privacy laws, offering stringent protection to personal data and imposing severe penalties for breaches.

India

India’s Information Technology Act (2000) regulates online activities, including e-commerce, cybercrime, and data protection, and includes provisions to deal with hacking and identity theft.
